Tourist Attractions  

Tourist Attractions

The Bean (Cloud Gate) Must see, it’s downtown in Millennium Park. This sculpture from the beginning of this century reflects Chicago’s skyline from the heart of Millenium Park. It’s right next to Crown Fountain and The Jay Pritzker Pavillion which are also nice sights. See it being built here
Willis Tower Skydeck (aka, Sears Tower) Best views of the city but they come at a cost. Long lines and high prices might not be worth them. They have protruding windows so you can "walk on air" but everything else looks (and is) a tourist trap. See "The Signature Lounge" on the Hancock Tower below for something similar that's more low key.
Architecture Boat Tour
  • Chicago Architecture Foundation (CAF) Tour ($44)
    This is arguably the better of them as it’s volunteers that love architecture that explain the city. It doesn’t run as late/early months as the others but it’s the recommended one
  • Wendella Architecture Tour ($39)
    Both Wendella and Shoreline do Tours that include multi-lingual headphones but are arguably not as good as the ones from the CAF.
  • Shoreline Architecture Tour ($37-$43)
  • Do not take a "Lake Tour", they are slightly cheaper but substantially less interesting
Navy Pier It's just here because you'll find it in most travel guides. It's super touristy and cheesy, avoid it at all costs.
Bike Tour along the Lakeshore The Lakefront Trail that is an extremely nice bike path that goes all the way north and south from Chicago between the city and the lake. Get a bike (there are several bike rental places next to Navy Pier) and go up to Castaways or Montrose beach and down to Adler Planetarium (you have to get off the trail next to Shedd Aquarium and get on the street to get to Adler). Adler planetarium has the best views of the skyline, highly recommended to go there, and it gets way better at night time.

Food  

Food

Deep Dish Pizza
  • Pequod's
    Arguably the best deep dish in Chicago although it's not in the most touristy-reachable areas of the city. (gets extra points because it's a couple of blocks from our place!)
  • Lou Malnati's
    Solid deep dish (they say their buttercrust is the best) with plenty of locations aroud the city
  • Gino's East
  • Giordanos
    Makes very good looking pizza but it's lacking in flavor compared to the other ones. It's the most advertised in turisty areas. Only go if in a pinch.
